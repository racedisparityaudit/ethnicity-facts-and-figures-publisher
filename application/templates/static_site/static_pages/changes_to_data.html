1. Background

The Ethnicity facts and figures website shows data about the experiences and outcomes of people from a variety of ethnic backgrounds. It includes over 180 pages of data (‘measure pages’). The website was launched alongside the Race Disparity Audit in October 2017, and is managed and updated by the Race Disparity Unit (RDU).

The focus of the RDU’s work has changed since the website was launched, from gathering and presenting data to producing internal briefings and analytical research reports. Our analysts have also supported the government’s COVID-19 response and the Commission on Race and Ethnic Disparities.

The RDU joined the Equality Hub in December 2020, along with the Disability Unit, Government Equalities Office and Social Mobility Commission.

The change of focus and working structure gives us the chance to develop a new strategy that:

  • balances our resources with the needs of our users
  • allows more flexible working
  • coordinates closely with other teams in the Equality Hub

All data currently provided on Ethnicity facts and figures will continue to be published by the relevant government department. With very few exceptions, it will also still be published on our website, though in some cases in a different format.

2. How we assessed the importance of measure page

We have assessed the importance of each measure page according to whether it:

  • is impacted by or important for the analysis of the COVID-19 pandemic
  • was highlighted or investigated by the Commission on Race and Ethnic Disparities
  • is in the ‘top 50’ measure pages, ranked by the number of page views
  • is in another high priority policy area

The criteria for prioritising and assigning publication levels will be reviewed regularly.

Not all measures can be updated annually, and some will be combined, which is why the total number of measures pages is lower than 180.

3. Proposal 1: use the assessment requirements to determine the extent of measure page updates

Some measure pages meet more than one of the assessment requirements, while others do not meet any.

We propose the following approach from September 2021.

If 2 or more assessment requirements are met

Measure pages that meet 2 or more of the assessment criteria will be fully updated as usual.

There are 42 pages in this group.

If one requirement is met

Measure pages that meet only one of the assessment requirements will be amended so they only present analysis by ethnicity (not other factors such as age, gender and geography).

There are 59 pages in this group.

In some cases, measure pages in this category already only have an analysis by ethnicity, and will therefore get a ‘full update’.

In some cases – for example, measure pages analysing pupil performance – it will be possible to combine the analyses by ethnicity from multiple measure pages into a single page.

If data is available by other factors – such as age, gender and geography – it will be included in the data download file. Any important findings from other factors will be included in the ‘Main facts and figures’ section.

If no requirements are met

Pages that do not meet any of the assessment criteria will only show an updated data download file. Tables, charts and commentary will be removed.

There are 54 pages in this group.

Again, it may be possible to combine pages – for example, either 2 or more data-only measures together, or a data-only measure with a full update.

See a full list of measure pages and their assessment criteria scores

New measure pages

For new pages, we will base the amount of information we provide on the same assessment criteria. For example, we may add a new page that is ‘data only’ because it does not meet any of the assessment criteria but it is of interest to policy colleagues or other stakeholders.

4. Proposal 2: stop publishing data that shows only 2 ethnic groups

The small sample sizes in many surveys do not allow for data to be shown for more than 2 ethnic groups.

Over 30 measure pages show at least some data for only 2 ethnic groups:

  • White, and Other than White
  • White British, and Other than White British

This type of data is not useful because it does not distinguish between diverse experiences within particular ethnic groups.

We propose that we stop publishing data shown for only 2 ethnic groups. This will affect:

  • 4 measure pages in the group that meets 2 or more assessment criteria
  • 9 measure pages in the group that meets 1 assessment criteria – including 6 pages that do not provide any additional breakdown
  • 16 measure pages in the group that do not meet any assessment criteria

We will work with government departments to see if it is possible for them to combine multiple years’ data to allow analysis by more than 2 ethnic groups.

5. Proposed timetable

We plan to make 2 sets of changes to Ethnicity facts and figures:

  • September to December 2021: we will update all ‘data only’ measure pages, and will combine measure pages where possible
  • January to March 2022: we will amend the group of measure pages that will include only analysis by ethnicity, and will combine measures where possible

‘Full update’ measures will be updated according to their normal schedule.

6. Questions

Please answer one or more of the following questions:

  1. How often should we review the criteria and re-prioritise measure pages?
  2. Are the 3 levels of publication acceptable in general?
  3. Is the proposal to combine some measure pages (in general) acceptable?
  4. For specific measure pages you are interested in, are the levels proposed acceptable? Is combining acceptable? In your response, please include the name of the measure and reasons why the proposed level is not acceptable.
  5. Is the proposal to stop publishing data that only shows 2 ethnic groups acceptable? Which measure pages should be prioritised for development? (For example, if multiple years’ data is combined, data could be shown for up to 18 ethnic groups.)
  6. Is the proposed timetable acceptable?
  7. Do you have any further comments or suggestions?

7. How to respond

Please send your responses by email to ethnicity@cabinetoffice.gov.uk.

The deadline for responses is 11:59pm on Sunday 22 August 2021.
